Castilleja

More commonly know as "Indian paintbrush" or "prairie-fire", castilleja is a native species to western north America that thrives in mountain valleys. It is a hemiparisitc plant that relies on surrounding grasses for nuetrients.


The bright-red color does not come from petals, but are actually bracts (specialized leaf-like structures). They are named after the Spanish botanist Domingo Castillejo, but the colloquial term "paintbrush" comes from these bracts, as the look like they have been carefully dipped with paint on their tips.
