Amaryllis

The Amaryllis Flower is a plant with heavy seeds that do not travel far, so they frequently are seen in very dense concentrations in the wild. They tend to grow after bushfires, as they are frequently suppressed in areas of taller plants.


This plant has a symiotic relationship with carpenter bees and noctuid moths in the wild. In horticulture, they have been cultivated into an especially large and vivid flower called the Amaryllis belladonna.
